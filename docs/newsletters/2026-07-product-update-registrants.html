  A few common questions

Using ZeroGEX

▸Where do I start — how should I actually use it?

Start free. The Gamma Levels pages show the three levels that matter most: the Gamma Flip (stabilizing vs. amplifying regime), and the Call Wall and Put Wall (your likely upside and downside friction). On a free trial you also get the live Dashboard and GEX Strike Profile in real time. The idea: the levels tell you where to pay attention; your own price action (VWAP, the opening-range break) tells you when.

▸Why might ZeroGEX show a different Gamma Flip or Net GEX than another platform?

Gamma calculations are highly methodology-dependent. Rather than treating each strike as a static snapshot, ZeroGEX models how dealer gamma exposure changes across a range of hypothetical underlying prices. The Gamma Flip is derived from where that modeled exposure crosses zero, while Net GEX represents the same curve at the current price.

Differences in expiration weighting, implied volatility, contract filtering, dealer-sign assumptions, and data timing can all produce different results between platforms. When the available chain is too thin or noisy to resolve a reliable level, ZeroGEX shows it as unresolved rather than presenting a potentially misleading number.

▸Can I replay or backtest the levels against historical price action?

Yes to both. Replay lets you scrub back through a past session and watch how price behaved around the Gamma Flip, Call Wall, and Put Wall as they moved — with a toggle to hide future candles so you're testing your read honestly. Backtesting runs a signal or rule against historical data with a full tearsheet.

▸Can I use it for futures like ES, MES, NQ, or MNQ?

Yes — the index and ETF options books can provide highly relevant structural context for futures. ES and MES traders can reference SPX and SPY levels, while NQ and MNQ traders can use QQQ today, with NDX coverage coming. The markets are closely related, but the levels aren't always a perfect one-to-one translation, since futures can trade at a basis to the underlying index or ETF.

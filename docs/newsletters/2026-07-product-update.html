Using ZeroGEX

Where do I start — how should I actually use the dashboard?

Start simple. On the Dashboard, read the three levels that matter most first: the Gamma Flip (are we in a stabilizing or an amplifying regime?), then the Call Wall and Put Wall (your likely upside and downside friction). Pull up the GEX Strike Profile to see how that structure sits on price — then confirm with your own price action: VWAP, the opening-range break, whatever you already trust. The levels tell you where to pay attention; your triggers tell you when. Don't try to watch all fourteen signals at once on day one.

Why am I seeing a different Gamma Flip or Net GEX than on other sites?

Because most sites take a shortcut we don't. The common method just adds up each strike's static gamma into a bar chart and calls the zero-crossing the “flip.” It's fast — but that flip tends to stick to whatever wall is in the snapshot, even when the true zero-gamma level has moved several percent away.

ZeroGEX computes the spot-shift dealer gamma profile instead — the original SpotGamma / SqueezeMetrics definition. We take the live option chain and re-price every contract's gamma across a grid of hypothetical prices around spot, apply the dealer sign convention (calls +, puts −), and weight by open interest and time to expiry so a single 0DTE spike can't hijack a multi-day level. Everything reads off that one curve: the Gamma Flip is where it crosses zero, and Net GEX at spot is its value at today's price — so the headline number and the regime can never contradict each other.

Two more reasons the numbers differ: we compute our own implied volatility and Greeks from live data in real time (not an end-of-day vendor snapshot), and when a chain is too thin or noisy to resolve a real flip, we show it as unresolved rather than silently freezing yesterday's level. Full write-up, with a vendor-by-vendor comparison, is at zerogex.io/guides/gamma-flip-calculation-before-vs-after.

Can I replay or backtest the levels against historical price action?

Yes to both. Replay lets you scrub back through a past session and watch how price behaved around the Gamma Flip, Call Wall, and Put Wall as they moved — with a toggle to hide future candles so you're testing your read honestly. Backtesting lets you run a signal or rule against historical data with a full tearsheet. Longer history, full-dashboard replay, and richer scenario testing are actively on the roadmap.

Can I trade futures like ES, MES, NQ, or MNQ with it?

Yes — you map the future to its index book. /ES and /MES trade off the SPX (and SPY) gamma levels; /NQ and /MNQ off QQQ today, with NDX coming. The structural levels are identical — only the price scale differs. When the cash index is closed, the Live Bulletin already shows the ES/NQ futures-implied price for context, and translating the levels directly into futures prices is on the roadmap.

== A FEW QUESTIONS I'VE BEEN GETTING ==

--- Using ZeroGEX ---

Q: Where do I start — how should I actually use the dashboard?
A: Start simple. On the Dashboard (https://zerogex.io/dashboard), read the
three levels that matter most first: the Gamma Flip (are we in a stabilizing
or an amplifying regime?), then the Call Wall and Put Wall (your likely upside
and downside friction). Pull up the GEX Strike Profile
(https://zerogex.io/gex-strike-profile) to see how that structure sits on
price — then confirm with your own price action: VWAP, the opening-range
break, whatever you already trust. The levels tell you WHERE to pay attention;
your triggers tell you WHEN. Don't try to watch all fourteen signals at once
on day one.

Q: Why am I seeing a different Gamma Flip or Net GEX than on other sites?
A: Because most sites take a shortcut we don't. The common method just adds
up each strike's static gamma into a bar chart and calls the zero-crossing
the "flip." It's fast — but that flip tends to stick to whatever wall is in
the snapshot, even when the true zero-gamma level has moved several percent
away.
   ZeroGEX computes the spot-shift dealer gamma profile instead — the
original SpotGamma / SqueezeMetrics definition. We take the live option chain
and re-price every contract's gamma across a grid of hypothetical prices
around spot, apply the dealer sign convention (calls +, puts −), and weight
by open interest and time to expiry so a single 0DTE spike can't hijack a
multi-day level. Everything reads off that one curve: the Gamma Flip is where
it crosses zero, and Net GEX at spot is its value at today's price — so the
headline number and the regime can never contradict each other.
   Two more reasons the numbers differ: we compute our own implied volatility
and Greeks from live data in real time (not an end-of-day vendor snapshot),
and when a chain is too thin or noisy to resolve a real flip, we show it as
unresolved rather than silently freezing yesterday's level. Full write-up,
with a vendor-by-vendor comparison:
https://zerogex.io/guides/gamma-flip-calculation-before-vs-after

Q: Can I replay or backtest the levels against historical price action?
A: Yes to both. Replay (https://zerogex.io/replay) lets you scrub back through
a past session and watch how price behaved around the Gamma Flip, Call Wall,
and Put Wall as they moved — with a toggle to hide future candles so you're
testing your read honestly. Backtesting (https://zerogex.io/backtesting) lets
you run a signal or rule against historical data with a full tearsheet. Longer
history, full-dashboard replay, and richer scenario testing are actively on
the roadmap.

Q: Can I trade futures like ES, MES, NQ, or MNQ with it?
A: Yes — you map the future to its index book. /ES and /MES trade off the SPX
(and SPY) gamma levels; /NQ and /MNQ off QQQ today, with NDX coming. The
structural levels are identical — only the price scale differs. When the cash
index is closed, the Live Bulletin (https://zerogex.io/live-bulletin) already
shows the ES/NQ futures-implied price for context, and translating the levels
directly into futures prices is on the roadmap.

--- Plans, billing & access ---

Q: When will I be charged, and how does the free trial work?
A: Both Basic and Pro start with a free trial (length is shown on the Pricing
page, https://zerogex.io/pricing). You're not charged during it — at the end

billing portal and you're never charged a cent.

Q: How do I switch or cancel my plan?
A: Both live in one place: Account -> Manage subscription (the Stripe billing
portal), https://zerogex.io/account. Switch Basic <-> Pro or monthly <->
annual there — upgrades and monthly -> annual apply immediately, downgrades
and annual -> monthly at period end, with proration on your next invoice.
Cancelling also takes effect at period end, so you keep full access until then
and your account isn't deleted. And if cost is the issue, just reply — I'd
rather find something that works than see you go.

Q: What's the difference between Basic and Pro?
A: Basic gives you the live Dashboard, the Live Bulletin, all metrics, the
strategy tools, and the Basic signals. Pro adds the Advanced signals, the
Composite Score, Backtesting, and programmatic API access. The full breakdown
is on the Pricing page (https://zerogex.io/pricing). (The free, 15-minute-delayed
Gamma Levels pages — SPX, SPY, QQQ — stay open to anyone, no signup:
https://zerogex.io/spx-gamma-levels)

Q: I'm on Pro — where's my API key?
A: API access is a Pro feature, but self-serve key generation isn't live

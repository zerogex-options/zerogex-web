== A FEW QUESTIONS I'VE BEEN GETTING ==

--- Using ZeroGEX ---

Q: Where do I start — how should I actually use the dashboard?
A: Start simple. On the Dashboard (https://zerogex.io/dashboard), read the
three levels that matter most first: the Gamma Flip (are we in a stabilizing
or an amplifying regime?), then the Call Wall and Put Wall (your likely upside
and downside friction). Pull up the GEX Strike Profile
(https://zerogex.io/gex-strike-profile) to see how that structure sits on
price — then confirm with your own price action: VWAP, the opening-range
break, whatever you already trust. The levels tell you WHERE to pay attention;
your triggers tell you WHEN. Don't try to watch all fourteen signals at once
on day one.

Q: Why might ZeroGEX show a different Gamma Flip or Net GEX than another
platform?
A: Gamma calculations are highly methodology-dependent. Rather than treating
each strike as a static snapshot, ZeroGEX models how dealer gamma exposure
changes across a range of hypothetical underlying prices. The Gamma Flip is
derived from where that modeled exposure crosses zero, while Net GEX
represents the same curve at the current price.
   Differences in expiration weighting, implied volatility, contract
filtering, dealer-sign assumptions, and data timing can all produce different
results between platforms. When the available chain is too thin or noisy to
resolve a reliable level, ZeroGEX shows it as unresolved rather than
presenting a potentially misleading number.
   Read the full methodology:
https://zerogex.io/guides/gamma-flip-calculation-before-vs-after

Q: Can I replay or backtest the levels against historical price action?
A: Yes to both. Replay (https://zerogex.io/replay) lets you scrub back through
a past session and watch how price behaved around the Gamma Flip, Call Wall,
and Put Wall as they moved — with a toggle to hide future candles so you're
testing your read honestly. Backtesting (https://zerogex.io/backtesting) lets
you run a signal or rule against historical data with a full tearsheet. Longer
history, full-dashboard replay, and richer scenario testing are actively on
the roadmap.

Q: Can I trade futures like ES, MES, NQ, or MNQ with it?
A: Yes — the index and ETF options books can provide highly relevant
structural context for futures. ES and MES traders can reference SPX and SPY
levels, while NQ and MNQ traders can use QQQ today, with NDX coverage coming.
The markets are closely related, but the levels aren't always a perfect
one-to-one translation, since futures can trade at a basis to the underlying
index or ETF. When the cash index is closed, the Live Bulletin
(https://zerogex.io/live-bulletin) already shows the ES/NQ futures-implied
price for context.

--- Plans, billing & access ---

Q: When will I be charged, and how does the free trial work?

Q: How do I switch or cancel my plan?
A: You can change or cancel your plan through Account -> Manage subscription

the change takes effect and whether any prorated adjustment applies before you

Q: What's the difference between Basic and Pro?
A: Basic gives you the live Dashboard, the Live Bulletin, all metrics, the

Composite Score, Backtesting, and programmatic API access. The full breakdown
is on the Pricing page (https://zerogex.io/pricing). (The free, 15-minute-delayed
Gamma Levels pages — SPX, SPY, QQQ — stay open to anyone, no signup:
https://zerogex.io/spx-gamma-levels)

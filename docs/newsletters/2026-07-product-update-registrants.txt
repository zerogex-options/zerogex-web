== A FEW COMMON QUESTIONS ==

--- Using ZeroGEX ---

Q: Where do I start — how should I actually use it?
A: Start free. The Gamma Levels pages (https://zerogex.io/spx-gamma-levels)
show the three levels that matter most: the Gamma Flip (stabilizing vs.
amplifying regime), and the Call Wall and Put Wall (your likely upside and
downside friction). On a free trial you also get the live Dashboard and GEX
Strike Profile in real time. The idea: the levels tell you WHERE to pay
attention; your own price action (VWAP, the opening-range break) tells you WHEN.

Q: Why might ZeroGEX show a different Gamma Flip or Net GEX than another
platform?
A: Gamma calculations are highly methodology-dependent. Rather than treating
each strike as a static snapshot, ZeroGEX models how dealer gamma exposure
changes across a range of hypothetical underlying prices. The Gamma Flip is
derived from where that modeled exposure crosses zero, while Net GEX represents
the same curve at the current price. Differences in expiration weighting,
implied volatility, contract filtering, dealer-sign assumptions, and data
timing can all produce different results between platforms. When the available
chain is too thin or noisy to resolve a reliable level, ZeroGEX shows it as
unresolved rather than presenting a potentially misleading number.
Read the full methodology:
https://zerogex.io/guides/gamma-flip-calculation-before-vs-after

Q: Can I replay or backtest the levels against historical price action?
A: Yes to both. Replay (https://zerogex.io/replay) lets you scrub back through
a past session and watch how price behaved around the Gamma Flip, Call Wall,
and Put Wall as they moved — with a toggle to hide future candles. Backtesting
(https://zerogex.io/backtesting) runs a signal or rule against historical data
with a full tearsheet.

Q: Can I use it for futures like ES, MES, NQ, or MNQ?
A: Yes — the index and ETF options books can provide highly relevant structural
context for futures. ES and MES traders can reference SPX and SPY levels, while
NQ and MNQ traders can use QQQ today, with NDX coverage coming. The markets are
closely related, but the levels aren't always a perfect one-to-one translation,
since futures can trade at a basis to the underlying index or ETF.

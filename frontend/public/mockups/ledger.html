§ II · The record, drawn

The flip, taken from today's book.

Plate I of the daily record. Positive dealer γ hatched above the axis; negative γ set in claret below.

Positive above.
Negative below.

The gamma flip is the single price at which dealer positioning changes signs. Above it, dealer books are long gamma — hedgers absorb volatility, ranges compress, and moves die on approach.

Below it, the machine runs the other way: hedgers must chase, ranges expand, and small impulses compound into session-defining candles before you finish the morning's first coffee.

"When the market is above the flip, hedge slower. When it is below, hedge sooner. There is no third instruction."

ZeroGEX draws this line, every fifteen seconds, from the option chain that is currently printing — not from a stale close, not from a proxy.

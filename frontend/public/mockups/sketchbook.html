Gamma flip.

The pivot at which dealer positioning changes sign. Above: stabilizing. Below: amplifying. Every intraday plan starts here.

Call & put walls.

The strikes carrying the largest dealer gamma — where the hedge is heaviest. Call wall above, put wall below, ranked by conviction.

Trap detector.

Retail stacked one way, dealers hedged the other. Historically the ugliest reversal setups on the chart — flagged before they happen.

skipped losses = kept wins.

Squeeze setup.

Composite of γ, skew, and vanna. Flags the regimes in which a small directional impulse compounds into a session-defining move.

low vol precedes big vol. always.

EOD pressure.

The final-hour re-hedging bias every closing print obeys. One hour of the day with a legible edge — even if you trade nothing else.

the last 60 minutes are legible.

Tape flow bias.

Directional read of aggressor flow, weighted by proximity to key walls. Answers "who's leaning on this move?" in a single line.

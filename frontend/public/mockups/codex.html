§ 0 · Abstract

A working specification
for reading dealer
positioning, live.
/ VOL. IV · MMXXVI

ZeroGEX defines a small, unambiguous set of live-computed instruments — the gamma flip, the walls, the trap detector — that together describe where dealer hedging obligations sit at any moment in the trading day. This document is their specification and their read-out.

All signals are computed from the full SPX and SPY option chain, refreshed at 250 ms, and rendered in a single reference chart. Definitions in § 1. Method in § 2. Reference implementation and API in § 3.

§ 1.1

The Gamma Flip FLIP · SPX

6,398

The single strike at which aggregate dealer gamma changes sign. Above · dealers are net long gamma and hedge against price direction; below · they are net short gamma and hedge with direction, amplifying the move.1

§ 1.2

Net Dealer GEX NET · SPX

−1.82B

The signed integral of dealer gamma over the whole chain. Signs the regime and sizes the conviction of a given session's positioning.

§ 1.3

Call Wall C-WALL · SPX

6,450

The strike carrying the largest short-call dealer γ. Historically a ceiling on advances; behaves as a magnet on approach, then as a lid on breach.

§ 1.4

Put Wall P-WALL · SPX

6,350

The strike carrying the largest short-put dealer γ. Historically a reflexive floor into declines.

§ 1.5

Positioning Trap TRAP · [0,100]

+82

Composite index of the divergence between retail positioning and dealer hedge polarity. High values flag the highest-conviction reversal setups historically observed.2

§ 1.6

Squeeze Setup SQZ · {LOW, MED, HIGH}

HIGH

A three-state composite of γ concentration, realized-vs-implied skew, and vanna sign. Flags regimes in which a small directional impulse compounds into a session-defining move.

§ 1.7

Tape Flow Bias TFB · [−100, +100]

−61

Directional read of aggressor flow, weighted by proximity to key walls. One-line answer to who is leaning on this move.

§ 1.8

End-of-Day Pressure EOD · {DIRECT, PIN}

DIRECT

Final-hour re-hedging bias. Historically the single hour of the trading day with a legible edge on the closing print.

1. See Christoffersen, Fournier & Jacobs (2018) on the reflexivity of dealer hedging under negative γ. Reproduced with modifications in ZG Codex Appendix A.

2. Composite formulation given in § 3.2. Reference implementation at /docs/trap.

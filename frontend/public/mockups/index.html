ZeroGEX / Design Studies

Twenty-four directions
for a website
with a spine.

Twenty-four fully-styled landing-page mockups. The final four (21–24) are hybrids of the two you liked most (Brutalist + Terminal Deluxe), warmed up and given room to breathe — walnut and slate instead of stark black/amber, thinner rules, more air, MCM warmth without literal MCM cosplay.

1. The Almanac

Editorial. Serif-heavy. Cream paper, oxblood ink, gold-leaf accents. A trading almanac you'd find in an old broker's library — dropped into 2026 with live data.

Open the Almanac

2. Terminal Deluxe

A love letter to the Bloomberg terminal, rebuilt for a phone. Deep navy-black, warm amber, CRT scanlines, blinking cursors, live-feel tables — dense but breathable.

JetBrains MonoCRTdense
Boot the Terminal

3. Chunky Cream

Warm cream, chunky bevelled buttons, hand-drawn line illustrations, rotated sticker badges. PostHog-adjacent, but built for gamma traders — with a gecko mascot pulling shift on the trading floor.

Fraunces 900hand-drawnplayful
Meet the Gecko

4. Brutalist

Swiss grid, oversized numerals, ruthless black borders, no rounded corners. The visual equivalent of "here's the number, do something with it." Confident, engineered, quiet.

Space Groteskgridsharp
Enter the Grid

5. Blueprint

Engineering-schematic aesthetic. Prussian blue ground, off-white hairlines, orange callouts, cyan levels. Every element labelled like it was drafted with drafting instruments — signals as parts in a catalog.

Open the Blueprint

6. Neo-Memphis

Playful geometric shapes, dusty terracotta + cobalt + lime, mixed serif italics with tight grotesk. Feels like a fintech that wants to be liked, not feared. Confetti behind the hero, sticker-note stats.

7. Sketchbook

Literal trader's notebook. Graph paper, ink pen, hand-lettered headers, red-pen annotations, yellow sticky notes, taped-in polaroids. Charts drawn with wobbly lines. Absurdly distinctive — feels artisanal.

8. Aurora

Very dark ground with iridescent violet + teal + magenta gradient wash. Frosted-glass panels, Playfair Display italic serif hero, JetBrains Mono labels. Reads as premium, sophisticated, quiet — Stripe-adjacent but pushed further.

9. Ledger

Bank certificate / private-banking aesthetic. Cream + hunter green + gold-leaf + claret. Copperplate seal, ruled ledger tables, guilloche borders. Feels like a walnut-paneled library and an annual report.

10. Wireframe

Isometric CAD render aesthetic — the option chain drawn as a 3D wireframe surface. Hairline neon lines on near-black, corner reticles, engineering annotations. Feels like a Rhino/Fusion export or a Bloomberg surface view.

11. Codex

Technical spec-document aesthetic — like an RFC or a Fed research paper. Serif body (Newsreader), formal § numbering, marginalia, footnotes, revision numbers. Reads as authoritative and quietly obsessive about precision.

12. Editorial

Monocle / FT Weekend magazine aesthetic. Ivory paper, deep petrol panels, coral accents, sand highlights. Fraunces serif with mono details. Asymmetric print rhythm — feels cosmopolitan, sophisticated, and unmistakably editorial.

13. Bauhaus

Herbert Bayer composition. Ivory paper, primary red/yellow/blue, black. Big geometric shapes as compositional elements — circle, triangle, square. Signal cards each sit on a distinct primary field. Feels like a 1923 poster.

Space Groteskprimarygeometric
Compose the Poster

14. Modulor

Le Corbusier polychromie palette — chalk + muted brick + ochre + deep petrol. Architectural grid, Modulor proportional bars, oversized numerals as building-scale signage. Restrained mid-century sophistication.

Inter TightModulorarchitectural
Enter the Modulor

15. Rothko

Meditative stacked color fields — maroon over ochre, petrol over burnt orange, cream over teal, cadmium over maroon. Type sits inside the fields. Room-by-room palette shifts as you scroll. Emotional and grand.

Inter Tightcolor-fieldmeditative

16. Serigraph

Warhol pop — held on a tight leash. Halftone dot fills, four-up repetition of the same signal in four colors, hot primary palette, drop-shadow blocks. Loud without being campy — mid-century pop with financial gravity.

17. Karesansui

Japanese rock garden. Warm off-white paper, a single stone as focal element, raked-sand curves in the background, moss accent. Content placed with the space it needs. Extreme restraint — Inter Tight 200 + Cormorant italic accents.

18. Case Study

Eames / Koenig house. Bone paper, walnut veneer panel, brushed-steel structural grid, soft avocado accent. Measurement panel floats on a steel column. Restrained architectural composition.

Braun / Dieter Rams record console. Warm cream + walnut veneer + brushed-aluminum faceplate + single amber LED accent. Numbers as VU meters, signals as knobs on a machined dial. Mellow analog precision.

Norm Architects / Aesop. Oat paper, sage green, terracotta, walnut. Cormorant Garamond serif italic + Inter Tight body. Very generous air. A vessel-like measurement card in the hero. Quiet luxury.

21. Brutalist Warm

Brutalist's structural grid discipline — same 12-col rigor and confident sans-serif — but warmed up. Warm slate instead of pure black, walnut brown accents, muted amber highlight, thinner 1px rules with more air.

Space Groteskwalnutgrid
Enter the Grid

22. Terminal Walnut

Terminal Deluxe evolved. Warm dark charcoal instead of pure black, cream text instead of stark white, walnut-amber accent instead of electric neon, muted sage/coral for pos/neg. Mellow but still terminal.

JetBrains Monowarm darkmellow
Boot the Terminal

23. Steel & Wood

Explicit hybrid: Brutalist's off-white paper base with Terminal's dark charcoal readout panels inset, walnut wood accents at the CTA. Structural grid + machined readout. The best of both, held together.

Space GroteskJetBrains Monohybrid
Open the House

24. Instrument

Refined Brutalist — thinner rules, oversized numerals as readouts, walnut as single-color accent, generous whitespace between sections. Fewer elements per screen, each one bigger. The confidence of Brutalist, the restraint of MCM.

Space Groteskoversizedrestrained
Read the Instrument
How to use these. These aren't full sites — each is a one-page landing that establishes the visual system so you can see how it feels. Pick the one that pulls you, and we can extend the palette + typography + component patterns across the rest of the marketing pages (pricing, education, signal pages).
